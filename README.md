# Most Visited Countries Data Analysis

## 1. Data Overview

### Size
The dataset consists of 207 rows in total (including 1 header row and 206 data rows for countries/territories) and 6 columns.

### Data Sources
Historical baselines are sourced from the World Bank, combined with international tourism statistics and predictive models for recent years.

### Context/Background
The dataset contains information about international tourist arrivals across different countries and territories. It includes historical tourism data, recent tourism performance, and predicted tourist arrivals for 2024. The analysis aims to explore tourism trends and identify important patterns in global travel activities.

---

## 2. Data Cleaning

### Missing Values
The dataset was reviewed to identify incomplete or empty values using Excel data cleaning tools. Records containing missing information were cleaned and adjusted to improve the accuracy and consistency of the dataset before conducting further analysis.

### Duplicate Rows
The dataset was also examined for duplicate entries to avoid repeated information. Duplicate records were removed to ensure each country would only appear once.

## 6. Key Insights

### Insight 1
The chart shows that Europe dominates tourism, with France leading at nearly 90 million visitors. There is a huge gap between the top three countries (France, Spain, and the USA) and the rest, meaning a few major destinations attract most international tourists. In addition, the rise of Middle Eastern countries and the absence of Asian countries suggest that global tourism preferences are changing rapidly.

### Insight 2
The chart shows that tourist arrivals increased across most countries from 2022 to 2023, with France leading the recovery at around 100 million visitors. This overall growth indicates that global tourism strongly recovered after pandemic travel restrictions were lifted. Furthermore, the noticeable increase in Saudi Arabia and the UAE highlights how the Middle East is becoming an increasingly competitive tourism region.

---

## 7. Conclusion

In conclusion, the data shows that global tourism recovered strongly from 2022 to 2023, and this growth is expected to continue into 2024. While Europe and the USA remain the top choices for international travelers, the rapid rise of Middle Eastern destinations suggests that the global tourism market is gradually evolving and becoming more competitive.
